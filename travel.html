Bali: the pause-button trip.

"The kind of place that resets you without you noticing it's happening."

Jungle mornings in Ubud, an island with no cars, cliffside temples, and a boat ride to Bali's wildest coastline — ten days, five bases, real notes from an October trip.

Best timeApril–October (dry season) — I went in October, mostly clear skies.
BudgetMid-range — stays ran $32–48/night, plus day tours and cafes.
Duration10 days across 5 bases.
📍 The postcard stuff

Jatiluwih's rice terraces at sunrise, Uluwatu Temple's cliffside sunset, Nusa Penida's cliffs in one wild day, Gili Trawangan's car-free pace — all worth it, all exactly as photographed. What I'd actually plan a return trip around is below.

Off the beaten path

Bukit TrunyanA hiking area up north almost nobody visiting Bali bothers with.
Goa GarbaA quiet cave and creek that barely shows up in reviews — exactly the appeal.
Lovina BeachDolphin-watching on the north coast — a totally different Bali from Canggu/Uluwatu.
El KabronA cliffside sunset spot in Uluwatu that isn't the one every itinerary sends you to.

Moments that stuck

🍜 Best meal

Sunset dinner at The Sayan House in Ubud — I still think about it.

☕ Favourite café

Crate Cafe near Uluwatu — smaller and quieter than anything on a "best cafes" list.

🌅 Favourite sunset

Gili T's west side — the whole island turns for it, but this side isn't the crowded one.

❤️ Would I return?

Yes — Nusa Penida and Ubud again, without hesitation.

A few tips

  • Book the Gili T fast boat ahead in high season — it sells out.
  • The Kecak Dance is touristy — do it once anyway.
  • Give Nusa Penida a full day. It's a ferry ride, not a quick stop.

Turkey: twelve days, eight stops, one road trip.

"History doesn't stay in museums here — it's in the marina cafés, the canyon trails, and the balloons overhead at sunrise."

Byzantine and Ottoman history in Istanbul, sunrise balloons over Cappadocia, ancient ruins down the Turquoise Coast, and Aegean towns to finish. A proper self-driven road trip — the real route I planned and drove in August.

Why I wentWanted one trip that covered history, nature, and coastline without switching countries — Turkey's rare in doing all three.
Best timeI went in August — hot on the coast, but Cappadocia mornings stayed cool for the balloon ride. April–June or Sept–Oct skips the peak heat.
BudgetMid-range — multi-currency trip, budget tracked by category (stay, food, misc) per stop.
Duration12 days across 8 stops.
📍 The must-dos

The sunrise balloon ride over Cappadocia's fairy chimneys, Ephesus and Pamukkale back to back, paragliding over Ölüdeniz's blue lagoon, crossing Europe to Asia over the Bosphorus in fifteen minutes — all of it lives up to the photos. The quieter half of the trip is below.

Off the beaten path

BalatIstanbul's colorful backstreets, a quieter contrast to the Grand Bazaar crowds.
Ihlara ValleyA canyon hike in Cappadocia dotted with rock-cut churches, well past where the balloon-tour crowds go.
Saklıkent CanyonIcy water and canyon trails near Fethiye that most coastal-town visitors skip.
AsansörA historic elevator in Izmir with a panoramic view — a quiet way to end the trip.

Moments that stuck

🎈 Best experience

The sunrise balloon launch over Cappadocia — hundreds of them lifting off at once.

☕ Favourite spot

Seaside cafes at Bodrum Marina and the promenade along Kordonboyu in Izmir — good coffee, better views.

🌉 Most memorable crossing

Istanbul, ferry deck, Europe behind me and Asia ahead — about fifteen minutes across the Bosphorus.

❤️ Would I return?

Yes — I'd slow down the coastal half and give Istanbul a couple more days.

A few tips

  • Rent a car for the coastal stretch — Antalya to Izmir doesn't work well without one.
  • Book the Cappadocia balloon slot the night before; weather cancels flights often.
  • Do Pamukkale and Ephesus as one combined day, not two separate stops.

📍 The names everyone knows

Ba Na Hills' Golden Bridge, Hoi An's lantern-lit old town, Fansipan's cable car, the Cu Chi Tunnels, Da Nang's Dragon Bridge — all worth the crowds. The parts I'd build a return trip around are quieter.

Off the beaten path

Cat Cat VillageA H'mong village near Sapa, a short walk from the cable car crowds.
Ta Van & Lao ChaiRice-terrace villages that most Fansipan day-trippers skip entirely.
Silver & Love WaterfallBoth far quieter than anything near the main Sapa strip.
The Lonely Tree of O Quy HoA single tree on a mountain pass that's become its own small pilgrimage.

Moments that stuck

🍜 Best meal

King Koi Coffee near Ho Chi Minh City — a fish-themed cafe that had no business being that good.

☕ Favourite café

The Cafe Apartment — an entire building of stacked cafes, pick a floor and a view.

🌧 Unexpected moment

Bui Vien Walking Street at night — chaotic once, memorable exactly because of it.

❤️ Would I return?

Yes — more time in Sapa's villages, less in Ho Chi Minh City.

A few tips

  • Do Ba Na Hills' Golden Bridge before 9am — tour buses arrive fast.
  • Book the Fansipan cable car early or expect a long queue.
  • Sapa in February is genuinely cold and often foggy — pack layers, don't count on views.

USA: a loop, not a line.

"Coastline to desert to alpine lake — the same trip, three completely different worlds."

Down the coast on Highway 1, out through the desert lights of Vegas, up into canyon and mountain country, and back through the Sierra — a road trip that starts and ends in San Francisco without ever repeating a road.

Why I wentWanted one drive that hit coastline, desert, and mountains without flying anywhere in between.
Best timeWarmer months work best — some high-elevation roads through Colorado and Utah close for snow otherwise.
BudgetMid-range — park fees, gas, and a mix of budget motels with a couple of splurge nights.
DurationOne big loop, coast to desert to mountains and back.
📍 The bucket-list stops

San Francisco's Painted Ladies and Palace of Fine Arts, Griffith Observatory and the Walt Disney Concert Hall in LA, Fremont Street lit up in Vegas, Rocky Mountain National Park and Red Rocks in Colorado — all exactly as advertised. The finds I'd send someone back for are quieter.

Off the beaten path

Seven Magic MountainsA quick, strange, brightly-painted detour right off the road into Vegas.
St. Mary's GlacierA short hike in Colorado to a genuine glacier, far quieter than the national park.
Photo Booth Museum by PhotomaticaA small, odd museum in San Francisco most itineraries never mention.

Moments that stuck

🍔 Favourite food

Arsicault Bakery in San Francisco, worth the line — and yes, In-N-Out is a real road-trip checkpoint, not a joke.

🏔️ Favourite stop

Rocky Mountain National Park into Hanging Lake — the day the "nature" part of the trip actually hit.

🎰 Most surreal moment

Seven Magic Mountains at dusk, neon rock stacks in the middle of the desert, twenty minutes off the highway.

❤️ Would I return?

Yes — I'd trade Colorado's extra time for more of Utah and Arizona.

A few tips

  • Book Rocky Mountain National Park's timed entry ahead in peak season — it sells out.
  • Hanging Lake requires a permit/reservation in season — check before you drive up.
  • This is a loop with real elevation changes — check mountain pass conditions before locking in a date.

Singapore: five to seven days, one city, no rush.

"A city so well-run it makes you wonder why anywhere else bothers being complicated."

A short trip, not a route — futuristic architecture, hawker food, and green space stitched into every block. Compact enough to cover well without ever feeling rushed.

Best timeTropical year-round — February to April tends to be the driest stretch.
BudgetMid-to-high — one of the pricier stops on this list, though transit keeps daily costs down.
Duration5–7 days, whole city.

Where I spent my time

Gardens by the BayThe Supertree Grove at night and the Cloud Forest by day.
Marina Bay Sands SkyParkThe skyline view the postcards use.
Chinatown & Little IndiaTwo completely different moods a short walk apart.
Sentosa IslandA beach-and-theme-park day off from the city.

Moments that stuck

🍜 Favourite food

Maxwell Food Centre and Lau Pa Sat — chili crab and Hainanese chicken rice at a fraction of restaurant prices.

🌆 Favourite view

The Supertree Grove lighting up at Gardens by the Bay, right as it gets dark.

❤️ Would I return?

Yes — easily one of the most efficient short trips on this list.

A few tips

  • Get an EZ-Link card on arrival — the MRT covers almost everything you'll want to see.
  • Time Gardens by the Bay for the evening light show, not just daytime photos.
  • Hawker centers are cash-and-queue — have small bills ready.

Thailand: five to seven days in Bangkok.

"Bangkok never really slows down — except right before the food does."

Temples, river life, and some of the best street food anywhere — a city that moves at double speed but rewards slowing down for the right meal.

Best timeNovember to February — cooler and drier than the rest of the year.
BudgetBudget-friendly — one of the best value cities on this whole list.
Duration5–7 days, Bangkok.

The essentials

Grand Palace & Wat Phra KaewThe obvious must-see, and worth the crowds.
Wat ArunAt sunset, best seen from across the river.
Chatuchak Weekend MarketThousands of stalls — easy to lose an afternoon.
Chao Phraya river boatTransport that doubles as sightseeing.

Moments that stuck

🍜 Favourite food

Street food stalls over sit-down restaurants — pad thai, mango sticky rice, whatever the longest local queue is pointing at.

🌇 Favourite view

Wat Arun at sunset, seen from across the river as the light hits the spires.

❤️ Would I return?

Yes — and next time I'd add an island leg onto the same trip.

A few tips

  • Dress modestly for temples — shoulders and knees covered, or you'll be turned away.
  • Bargain at markets; the first price is never the real one.
  • Grab is more predictable than street-hailed tuk-tuks for longer distances.

UAE: five to seven days in Dubai.

"Fifteen minutes can take you from the tallest building on Earth to a spice souk that hasn't changed in decades."

Record-breaking buildings, desert on the edge of the city, and a skyline that seems to change every year — a short trip that swings between hyper-modern and old-town in the same afternoon.

Best timeNovember to March — before the summer heat makes daytime plans difficult.
BudgetMid-to-high — costs add up fast around the Marina and Downtown areas.
Duration5–7 days, Dubai.

The big-ticket stuff

Burj KhalifaObservation deck, timed for sunset if you can get the slot.
Dubai Mall & FountainThe show right outside it.
Desert safariDune bashing by day, a Bedouin-style camp dinner after dark.
Old Dubai's souksSpice and gold markets across the creek from the modern skyline.

Moments that stuck

🍢 Favourite food

Arabic mezze and shawarma in Old Dubai — smaller restaurants near the souks beat almost anything closer to the Marina.

🏜️ Best experience

The desert safari — dune bashing by day, a Bedouin-style camp dinner once the sun went down.

❤️ Would I return?

Yes — I'd trade a day in the Marina for more time in Old Dubai.

A few tips

  • Book desert safaris a day ahead — the good operators fill up.
  • Dress code matters more in Old Dubai and at religious sites than in the Marina.
  • Avoid outdoor plans in peak summer heat — this is a cooler-season city trip.
