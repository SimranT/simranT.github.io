Itinerary · Turkey Road Trip

One road, eight stops, all of Turkey's range.

This was a proper road trip — Byzantine and Ottoman history in Istanbul, sunrise balloons over Cappadocia, ancient ruins down the Turquoise Coast, and Aegean towns to finish. Built from the actual route I planned and drove in August.

Istanbul→Cappadocia→Antalya→Kaş→Fethiye→Pamukkale & Ephesus→Bodrum→Izmir
The route

City by city

Istanbul5 days

History, bazaars, and two continents

  • Hagia Sophia & the Blue Mosque — the obvious must-sees, and worth every bit of the hype.
  • Topkapi Palace & Basilica Cistern — Ottoman history above ground, Byzantine engineering below it.
  • Grand Bazaar & İstiklal — one for bargaining, one for people-watching and Beyoğlu's energy.
  • Galata Tower for panoramic views, plus day trips to Adalar (Princes' Islands) and the colorful streets of Balat.
Istanbul is the only city in the world that sits on two continents — cross the Bosphorus and you've gone from Europe to Asia in about 15 minutes.
Cappadocia2 days

Sunrise balloons and underground cities

  • Hot air balloon at sunrise — the reason most people come, and it delivers.
  • Göreme Open Air Museum — rock-cut churches with frescoes still intact.
  • Kaymaklı Underground City and Uçhisar Castle for the region's strangest history.
  • Ihlara Valley — a hike along a canyon dotted with more rock-cut churches.
Cappadocia's "fairy chimneys" formed over millions of years as wind and rain eroded soft volcanic rock, leaving harder caps balanced on softer pillars.
Antalya1 day

Ottoman harbor, ancient ruins

  • Kaleiçi Yat Limanı — the old harbor, Ottoman houses, and Hadrian's Gate.
  • Düden Waterfalls cascading straight into the Mediterranean.
  • Aspendos Theater and Perge Ancient City if ruins are your thing — both well preserved.
Antalya's old harbor, Kaleiçi, has been in continuous use since Hellenistic times — over 2,000 years of ships docking in the same bay.
Kaş1 day

Quiet coast, turquoise water

  • Kaş Old Town — pedestrian streets that feel like a different country from Istanbul entirely.
  • Kaputaş Beach — the turquoise-water cliff beach you've seen in photos.
  • Küçük Çakıl for a beach within walking distance of town when you don't want to drive.
Kaş sits so close to Greece that the island of Meis (Kastellorizo) is clearly visible from town, just a few kilometers offshore.
Fethiye1 day

Blue lagoon and hiking trails

  • Ölüdeniz — the famous blue lagoon, crystal clear and worth the crowds.
  • Butterfly Valley — a secluded beach reachable by boat or a proper hike.
  • Saklıkent Canyon for icy water and canyon trails, plus a stretch of the Lycian Way.
Ölüdeniz's blue lagoon is one of the most paraglided-over spots on Earth — jumpers launch from Babadağ mountain, 1,900 metres above the beach.
Pamukkale & Ephesusday trip

Travertines and one of the best-preserved ancient cities

  • Pamukkale — the white travertine terraces, plus the Hierapolis ruins right above them.
  • Ephesus — the Library of Celsus and the ancient theater, genuinely one of the best-preserved ancient cities anywhere.
  • House of the Virgin Mary and the Basilica of St John nearby if you have extra time.
Pamukkale means "cotton castle" in Turkish — named for its white travertine terraces, built up by mineral-rich hot springs over thousands of years.
Bodrum1 day

Castle, marina, and Aegean cafes

  • Bodrum Castle and its Museum of Underwater Archaeology.
  • Mausoleum at Halicarnassus — one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, in ruins but still worth seeing.
  • Bodrum Marina for a slow evening and seaside cafes, plus the iconic windmills overlooking the bay.
The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus in Bodrum was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World — the word "mausoleum" comes directly from its name.
Izmir1 day

The last stop — Aegean city life

  • Kordonboyu — a promenade along the Aegean Sea, cafes and all.
  • Konak for the Clock Tower and Ottoman architecture, and the Agora Open Air Museum for ancient Greek ruins.
  • Asansör — a historical elevator with panoramic views to end the trip on.
Izmir was known as Smyrna for most of its history and is considered one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, dating back over 8,000 years.
