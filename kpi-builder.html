How it's built

The thinking behind the builder

01

Objective first, industry second

The 24 KPIs are organized by what you're trying to achieve, not by industry — industry only changes the schema and framing, not the underlying logic.

02

Every metric has a watchout

A KPI without a caveat is how dashboards mislead people. Each card includes the way this specific number tends to get misread.

03

SQL as a starting point

These queries won't run against your actual warehouse unchanged — they're a structurally correct starting point for the shape of the problem.

04

Layout before design

The blueprint answers "what goes where" before anyone opens a BI tool — the layout decision, made first, on purpose.

What this taught me

A few lessons baked into this lab

A KPI is an answer to a question, not a number

"What's our churn rate" means nothing until you know why you're asking — growth, retention, and profitability all care about different numbers.

More metrics isn't more clarity

Six well-chosen KPIs beat twenty scattered ones. The hard part is deciding what not to track.

The watchout matters more than the formula

Almost any KPI can be gamed or misread. Knowing how is what separates someone who reports a number from someone who understands it.

Schema shapes thinking

The same business question looks different once you have to name the actual tables and columns involved — it forces precision that a KPI name alone doesn't.
