How it's built

The thinking behind the explorer

01

One shared trip state

Every destination card, chart, and map pin reads from the same underlying array — add or edit one place and everything downstream updates together.

02

Real vs. illustrative, labeled clearly

Currency rates are live and real. Seasonal pricing and hotel data are illustrative — and the page says so, instead of blurring the line.

03

Comparison over single numbers

A single cost estimate is easy to ignore. Seeing four side by side is what actually helps someone decide.

04

No dead ends

Once you've picked a destination, the natural next step is tracking the real trip — which is exactly what the Trip Budget Planner does.

What this taught me

A few lessons baked into this lab

Flights anchor the decision more than people expect

For short trips, the flight can be the single biggest lever — bigger than picking a "cheap" destination with an expensive flight to reach.

Averages hide the expensive days

A daily average smooths over the one splurge day. Real trip tracking (not exploring) needs day-by-day logging — that's a different tool for a different job.

Shoulder season is usually the real answer

Not the cheapest month, not the busiest — the month right before or after peak is where price and experience are both still good.

Comparison beats research paralysis

Ten browser tabs of blog posts is worse than three destinations side by side on one screen. Structure beats volume.
