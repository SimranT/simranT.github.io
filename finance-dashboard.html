How it's built

The thinking behind the dashboard

01

Planning

Started with the question a real client would ask: "am I financially healthy?" — then worked backward to the inputs needed to answer it.

02

Data model

One input layer (income, expenses, subscriptions, debt, savings, investments) feeding a calculation layer that never touches raw entry fields directly.

03

Formula logic

Net worth, savings rate, FIRE number, and a weighted health score — each formula named, testable, and explainable in one sentence.

04

Automation & charts

Everything recalculates on input, no "submit" button required — the same live-refresh feel as a proper BI dashboard.

What I'd tell you at work

A few lessons this dashboard is built on

One score beats ten numbers

Most people don't act on 12 metrics. They act on one clear signal. That's why the health score exists — it's a summary, not a replacement, for the detail underneath.

The 50/30/20 rule is a benchmark, not a rule

50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings is a useful default — not a law. The point is knowing where you stand against it, then deciding on purpose to deviate.

Your FIRE number moves when your spending does

Financial independence is 25x your annual expenses, not your income. Cut spending and the target gets closer from both directions at once.

Cash flow is the number that catches problems early

Net worth can look fine while cash flow quietly goes negative every month. This dashboard surfaces cash flow on its own so it can't hide.
