Itinerary · Bali

10 days, five bases, one real trip.

Jungle mornings in Ubud, an island with no cars, cliffside temples, a boat trip to Bali's wildest coastline, and cafe-hopping to close it out. Built from my own notes and saved places from an October trip.

Ubud→Gili Trawangan→Uluwatu→Nusa Penida→Canggu
The route

Base by base

Ubud3 days

Jungle air and a slower pace

  • Jatiluwih Rice Terraces in the morning light, then Pura Tirta Empul for the holy spring water ritual.
  • Waterfall day — Sekumpul was the standout, with Tibumana, Kanto Lampo, and Tukad Cepung as backups if you have time.
  • Dinner at The Sayan House for a proper sunset over the valley.

Stayed: Suite Room with Mountain View — ~$48/night

Ubud's Sacred Monkey Forest is home to over 1,200 long-tailed macaques and three centuries-old temples — all inside a single patch of jungle in the middle of town.
Gili Trawangan2 days

No cars, no scooters, no rush

  • Fast boat transfer from Bali — book ahead in high season.
  • Snorkeling straight off the beach, plus a full loop of the island by bike.
  • Sunset from the west side of the island is the one not to miss.

Stayed: Kecamatan Pemenang, Gili T — ~$32/night

None of the three Gili Islands allow cars or motorbikes — the only ways around are on foot, by bike, or by cidomo, a traditional horse-drawn cart.
Uluwatu2 days

Cliffs, temples, and sunset crowds

  • Uluwatu Temple, Suluban Beach, and Melasti Beach across a couple of unhurried days.
  • The Kecak Dance at Melasti Beach at sunset — touristy, but worth it once.
  • Iron Fairies or Savaya after, if you want a nightcap with a view.
Uluwatu Temple sits on a 70-metre cliff and is one of Bali's six key directional temples, believed to guard the island from evil sea spirits.
Nusa Penida1 day

A day trip to Bali's wildest coastline

  • Paluang Cliff for the kind of viewpoint that doesn't look real.
  • Tembeling Beach and Forest — a forest walk that opens onto a hidden beach.
  • Pasih Andus (Smoky Beach), named for the sea spray that looks like smoke against the cliffs.
Nusa Penida's coastline was carved by strong ocean currents, which is why most of its famous spots are dramatic viewpoints rather than swimming beaches.
Canggu2 days

Cafes, surf, and a slow finish

  • Cafe-hopping at Suburbia Cafe, AKASA, and Paperhills.
  • A look around Nuanu Creative City if you want something different from beach clubs.
  • Close to the airport, which makes it the easiest last stop before flying out.
Canggu was mostly rice fields and quiet surf breaks until the 2010s — it's now one of Bali's fastest-growing digital nomad hubs.
